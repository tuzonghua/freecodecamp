Sonny Rollins

Saxophone Colossus

Sonny Rollins at the San Francisco Opera House Feb. 22, 1982.

Here's a timeline of Sonny Rollins' life:

  • 1930 - Born in New York City
  • 1946 - After beginning as a pianist, then alto sax player, finally switches to tenor sax
  • 1949 - Makes first recordings with bebop singer Babs Gonzales
  • 1951-53 - Records with Miles Davis, Modern Jazz Quartet, Charlie Parker, and Thelonious Monk
  • 1954 - Records standards "Oleo", "Airegin", and "Doxy" with Miles Davis
  • 1955 - Briefly joins Miles Davis Quintet and Clifford Brown-Max Roach quintet
  • 1956 - Records Saxophone Colossus, a milestone of Rollins' discography
  • 1956 - Marries actress and model Dawn Finney
  • 1956 - Records Tenor Madness
  • 1957 - Begins experimenting with "strolling", the use of bass and drums, without piano, as accompaniment
  • 1958 - Appears in Art Kane's famous photograph A Great Day In Harlem
  • 1958-61 - Takes a sabbatical from recording
  • 1959 - First tour of Europe
  • 1962 - Records "comeback" album The Bridge
  • 1963 - First tour of Japan
  • 1969-71 - Takes a second sabbatical
  • 1972 - Receives a Guggenheim Fellowship
  • 1979 - Plays unaccompanied on The Tonight Show
  • 1983 - Honored as "Jazz Master" by the National Endowment for the Arts
  • 1995 - November 13 declared "Sonny Rollins Day" by New York City
  • 1997 - Voted "Jazz Artist of the Year" by Down Beat magazine
  • 2001 - Wins Grammy Award for Best Jazz Instrumental Album for This Is What I Do
  • 2006 - Wins Grammy Award for Jazz Instrumental Solo for "Why Was I Born?"
  • 2006 - Wins Down Beat Readers Poll "Jazzman of the Year", "#1 Tenor Sax Player", and "Recording of the Year" for Without a Song: The 9/11 Concert
  • 2010 - Awarded National Medal of Arts and Edward MacDowell Medal
  • 2013 - Received honorary Doctor of Music degree from Julliard School
  • 2015 - Received Jazz Foundation of America's lifetime achievement award
